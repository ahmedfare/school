Since assuming the reins of power in the country in 2014, President El-Sisi began drawing up a giant development map for Egypt, as the period of President El-Sisi’s rule was marked by the launch of giant national projects such as (the new Suez Canal, the administrative capital, the New Alamein, and the fight against terrorism). The president continues construction, development, and the establishment of national projects. Great, as the president has brought about a major development renaissance since assuming power, and we monitor the most important information about the Egyptian state’s achievements during the 8 years:

New Administrative Capital

New Cairo capital city is located 35 KM east of Cairo of a total area of 170,000 feddan. The new capital city lies between the regional ring road, the Cairo-Suez road and Cairo-El Ain El Sokhna road. The new capital city will help to strengthen and diversify the country’s economic potential by creating new places to live, work and visit. In order to draw people to this new capital city, a series of key catalyst developments will be established at its core. This will include a new government administrative district, a cultural district and a wide variety of urban neighbourhoods.

New Alamein

New Alamein lies on the Mediterranean Sea, approximately 85 km west of Borg El Arab International Airport. Still under construction, New Alamein was conceived by the Egyptian government as a center for tourism, education, and government. The city has its own presidential palace and ministry building.

Streets,Bridges

Egypt's roads and bridges construction has been constantly growing for the aim of an effective and eased traffic flow across Egypt, this shall put an end to the congestion and overcrowding in several neighborhoods. The projects include infrastructure, upgrading and widening and all necessary services to achieve traffic smoothness. GIECO, being one of the main EPC companies, has been part of Egypt's roads and bridges expansion plan. With more than 15 roads and bridges being delivered, GIECO continues its growth with 5 more bridges still under construction with an estimated completion date by December 2023.
